Israel's Exceptional Fertility

A Global Outlier in the Developed World

Damir Zhumatayev | KAIST

1. Global Context

  • Israel's Total Fertility Rate (TFR): 3.0 children per woman
  • Highest among OECD countries
  • Well above replacement level of 2.1
  • Contrast: South Korea's TFR is 0.81

2. Pro-Natalist Policies

  • Universal healthcare coverage for expectant mothers
  • 15 weeks of paid maternity leave, funded by the government
  • Up to $49,500 in government support for a family with 4 children
  • High female employment rates alongside high fertility

3. Cultural and Religious Influences

  • Religious values promote family growth: "Be fruitful and multiply"
  • High fertility rates among religious groups: TFR of 3.77 (religious) and 6.38 (Ultra-Orthodox)
  • Secular traditionalism: 90% attend Passover seders, 70% celebrate Shabbat
  • Intergenerational project: Children seen as continuation of national story
